Process section — anchoring variations

Four directions to fix the "header floating, body floating" feeling. Each shows desktop (left) + mobile (right) so you can see the full picture.

A Unified card

Each stage is a single white card containing everything — number, title, body, focus areas, outcome. Internal divider line separates the head row from the body. Solid container that anchors all the stage content as one piece.

My read:
  • A · Unified card — the most direct fix. Everything's in one container, so there's no "floating" feeling. Safe and clean. Trade-off: the white card is a strong visual element repeated 3 times in a row.
  • B · Sage left border — same as A but with the brand color anchoring the left edge. The green stripe gives the section a rhythmic vertical heartbeat. My pick.
  • C · Timeline rail — most distinctive. Reads as a real process timeline with circular markers and a connecting line. Trade-off: bigger structural change, more "designed."
  • D · Two-tone card — combines the unified container with a sage band at the top to pace each stage. Most editorial. Trade-off: introduces a 4th surface tone (pastel sage on the stage head).
