SprintAssembly · Section Tinting Exploration

Three intensities, same content, same site.

Each of the three scrollable previews below renders the full page (hero + opportunity + roadmap + team + linkedin + contact) with a different section-tinting intensity applied. The LinkedIn cream signature stays the same across all three. The question is how much the other sections should differentiate themselves by background color as you scroll.

Option 1

Subtle — sections whisper, not speak

Each section gets a very slight background tint (2–5% opacity away from near-white). You'd barely notice them individually, but scrolling gives a faint sense of rhythm. Most conservative extension of the brief.

Trade-off: might be so subtle that it fails to address the "flat" concern. You'll see sections shift but might not feel the dynamic lift you're after.

Option 2

Medium — sections visibly differentiated

Each section has a distinct-but-still-calm tint. Opportunity warms into light amber, Roadmap cools into light teal, Team stays warm white, LinkedIn stays cream, Contact settles into neutral warm. Clear rhythm, still composed.

My recommendation. Adds real color dynamic without abandoning restraint. The tint language becomes: teal = systems/process, amber = business/outcomes, cream = voice/editorial, neutral = humans. That's an actual system.

Option 3

Bold — sections read as chapters

Each section gets a saturated tint. Hero is washed teal+amber. Opportunity is confident cream/amber. Roadmap is distinct teal. Team drops to neutral warmth. LinkedIn stays cream. Contact settles into a warm neutral to close. Reads like an editorial chapter book, not a scrolling page.

Trade-off: visually distinct per section, but leans less "unfussy." Works if you want the site to feel more like Stripe Press / a print magazine and less like a standard SaaS landing page.

How to pick

1 (Subtle) — you sense rhythm without noticing specific colors. Safest, least change. Probably won't move your "flat" feeling needle much.

2 (Medium) — recommended. Clear per-section identity that tells a color story: teal for process/systems, amber for business/outcomes, cream for editorial voice, neutral for humans. Extensible design language.

3 (Bold) — each section reads as its own chapter. More editorial, more print-magazine. Loudest; may push past "unfussy."

Note: these previews are at ~900px width with simplified copy. The real site at 950px container will look similar. LinkedIn cream bg stays in all three as your existing signature.
