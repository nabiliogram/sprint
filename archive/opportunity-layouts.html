SprintAssembly · Opportunity Section

Three ways out of the 3-card grid.

Each specimen below shows the Opportunity section — eyebrow, title, italic intro, and the three benefits — rendered with a different layout. Same copy in all three; the argument is that layout changes how the three items relate to each other and whether they feel like a diagram or a list. Read each, then tell me which one you want to ship.

Option A

Primary + two supporting

PRODUCTIVITY becomes the featured card (full-width, larger typography, generous padding). PERFORMANCE and PROFIT drop below as two supporting cards. The layout argues that productivity is the lead outcome and the other two are its consequences.

Trade-off: commits to a hierarchy. If all three are equally important to your pitch, this layout overstates PRODUCTIVITY. Natural fit since the hero already sells "agentic scale → reclaim capacity."
Business Impact

The Opportunity

You can't scale through manual effort alone. We help you transition to autonomous systems that target the three essentials of modern business: reclaiming team bandwidth, making your operations responsive to real-time signals, and improving your bottom line through intelligent orchestration.

Productivity

Scale your business without hiring more people.

Eliminate the manual bottlenecks slowing you down. By automating data integration and deploying agents across repetitive workflows, we build a standardized operating model that scales. Your organization increases total output without linear increases in headcount.

Performance

Real-time decisions across every workflow.

Our autonomous systems react to operational signals the moment they happen — an underperforming ad set, a support queue backing up, a pipeline stage going cold.

Profit

Increase profitability through smart orchestration.

Turn your business data into an intelligent orchestration layer. The result is an operating model that's both predictable and profitable.

Option B

Editorial asymmetric columns

Three columns with deliberately different widths (5 / 3 / 4 of 12) — PRODUCTIVITY widest as lead, PERFORMANCE most compact, PROFIT mid-width. Each column starts with a wide-tracked numeral and a mossy-green rule. Reads like a magazine contents page. No card containers — just columns of typeset information.

Trade-off: less visually distinctive than cards, but arguably more confident. Works if typography is carrying the weight. Loses the tag-chip / icon visual rhythm — relies on text rhythm instead.

Option C

Index / catalogue

Three full-width horizontal rows inside a single white container, divided by thin rules. Each row has a large mossy-green numeral and full-width prose to the right. Reads like a well-typeset table of contents or an instruction manual index. Maximum restraint.

Trade-off: no card-to-card visual rhythm — it's one long list. Reads as more "serious" / less "marketing." Best fit if the "unfussy" axis dominates; might feel austere if you want more color energy in this section.

How to pick

A if you want the argument "Productivity is the headline; Performance and Profit are its implications." Most argumentative.

B if typography + asymmetric space should do the work instead of cards — a more editorial / magazine reading of the section. Most restrained with the most rhythm.

C if you want the Opportunity section to read as an indexed catalogue rather than a sales section. Most "unfussy" per the brief. Loses color energy but gains composure.
