Option A

Public Sans

Single family · Free (Google Fonts)
One typeface handles display and body; hierarchy done via weight and size. Designed for the US Web Design System — purpose-built neutral, readable, civic.

Trade-off: restrained to a fault. You get composure; you give up personality. Ships fastest, zero licensing.

Option B

Archivo Black + Public Sans

Two families · Free (Google Fonts)
Heavy-weight display (Archivo Black 900) for h1s and section titles; neutral body (Public Sans). Museum-catalogue feel — headlines feel punched into the page.

Trade-off: Archivo Black ships at ONE weight (900). No lighter display moment available without adding a third face.

Option C

Onest

Single family · Free (Google Fonts)
A newer modernist grotesque with tighter apertures and slightly more mechanical character than Public Sans. The closest freely-available proxy to the Söhne / ABC Diatype territory the brief actually names.

Trade-off: less battle-tested than Public Sans (newer release). Renders beautifully at display sizes; body is good but not as quietly competent as Public Sans.

How to pick

A if you want the site to feel civic and composed — the font disappearing so the content carries.

B if you want the hero and section titles to land harder visually — the typography arguing the claim before the copy does.

C if you want more character than A without paying for D — a middle path.

D if you want the site to feel like it belongs in the same typographic neighbourhood as Stripe Press / Linear / OpenAI. The brief's top ceiling.
