# SprintAssembly — Design Context

This file captures the design direction for the SprintAssembly site. Future design work on this project should load this context before writing any code or visual decisions.

## Design Context

### Users

The site reads to two overlapping audiences, and needs to work for both:

- **Enterprise CMOs and marketing VPs** evaluating whether to bring in a specialist consultancy. They arrive skeptical of "agency-speak" and are scanning for evidence of rigour — named methodology, specific outcomes, real client logos.
- **Hands-on growth and ops leads at $10M–$100M scaleups** looking for help modernising how their business runs, without adding headcount. They want proof the team can actually ship and knows tooling, not just deliver slide decks.

In practice this means strong information scent in section titles, scannable eyebrow labels for the skim-reader, and the ability to go deeper (frameworks, process spine, case specifics) without the page losing its composure.

Primary job-to-be-done: *help me decide whether these people are credible and whether they could help me unlock capacity without adding headcount.*

Note: as of 2026-04-17 the site's positioning broadened from performance-marketing-specific → **agentic workflow automation**. Marketing sits as a proof point, not the frame.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: Rigorous · Pragmatic · Unfussy.**

- **Rigorous** — the work is data-led and the design should feel earned. No "transformative synergy." Numbers, methods, and named processes over vibes.
- **Pragmatic** — useful before clever. Every element justifies its existence. This is engineers-who-do-operations, not consultants-who-like-engineering.
- **Unfussy** — restraint as a stylistic position. The site shouldn't feel like it's trying. Confident enough to leave things alone. Not austere — just adult.

Adjacent references: Klim Type specimens, Linear's marketing, Stripe Press, Bravo Bravo, a McKinsey deck with the bloat removed. Dry, grown-up-confident.

Emotional goal for the reader: *"these people are for real."* Not dazzled, not charmed — trusted.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Refined and restrained.** Minimalism as a signal of confidence, not as a safety position. The craft lives in typography, spacing, and micro-details. Visual effects are earned, never decorative.

#### Keep and double down on

- **Warm near-white background** with the soft teal + amber radial wash on off-white. Already differentiates sharply from consultancy-grey — this is a signature surface.
- **Mossy green (#afb975)** as the single accent. Powerful because it's rare; don't dilute it by using it on every element.
- **Black "highlight" treatment on section titles** (`bg-neutral-950 px-4 py-1 text-white` with `box-decoration-clone`) — this is the site's most memorable visual move. Keep it and use it only on top-level section titles.
- **Sharp-cornered buttons** (`rounded-sm`) paired with softer cards (`rounded-2xl`). The contrast reads as intentional — keep it.
- **Wide-tracked uppercase eyebrows** at 11px with `tracking-[0.28em]`. They carry the "indexed, catalogued" feel that fits the "assembly" metaphor.
- **LinkedIn insights section** as custom branded cards (2026-04-17 rewrite) rather than iframe embeds — much better fit for the brand.

#### Upgrade

- **Typography.** Current body stack is Inter (imported in `src/index.css`), which is the #1 banned reflex font. Replace with a deliberate display/body pairing. Audition faces with genuine opinion — grotesques with character (Söhne, Matter, ABC Diatype, Favorit, NB International, Untitled Sans), or if going editorial, a text face like GT Sectra or Söhne Schmal for display. Reject reflex defaults: Inter, IBM Plex, Fraunces, Instrument Sans, DM Sans, Space Grotesk.
- **Line-length discipline.** Cap body paragraphs at ~65–75ch. Current site lets prose run too wide in places.
- **Rhythmic spacing.** Vary section padding to create pace — tight groupings, generous breaks — instead of evenly-distributed block-after-block.
- **Hierarchy contrast.** Increase the ratio between heading sizes and body text. At least 1.25× between steps of the scale.

#### Theme

**Light.** The warm near-white surface is correct for this audience and tone — daytime, consultative, unfussy. Do not switch to dark.

#### Anti-references — what this must NOT look like

- **Consultancy grey** — navy-and-grey palette, Times New Roman knockoffs, overly formal, PowerPoint-adjacent.
- **AI-startup futurism** — dark mode with neon or cyan accents, mesh gradients, glowing orbs, "future is here" copy, purple-to-blue gradients.
- **Generic SaaS / B2B template** — Inter font, Lottie animations, stock isometric illustrations, uniform icon-grid feature sections, gradient headlines.

### Design Principles

1. **Earn every element.** If it doesn't carry weight — information, hierarchy, or signature character — cut it. The site should feel edited, not assembled.

2. **Signature over novelty.** The mossy green, the warm wash, the black-highlight section titles, the wide-tracked eyebrows — these are the brand. Commit to them everywhere; don't introduce competing visual ideas. Consistency reads as confidence.

3. **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Where generic sites lean on colour, gradient, and illustration, SprintAssembly leans on type. Scale, weight, tracking, and pairing are the primary expressive tools.

4. **Rhythm over uniformity.** Vary spacing, section size, and density intentionally. The page should have pace — not the metronome tick of equal padding block after block.

5. **Evidence before adjective.** Named frameworks, numbered steps, specific outcomes, concrete nouns. The design should reflect the same instinct as the copy — precise, literal, load-bearing.
